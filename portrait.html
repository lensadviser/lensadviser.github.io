Best Portait Lenses

we researched various lenses and compared them based on factors such as focal length, aperture, sharpness, bokeh, user reviews, intended use, and budget. Here is the list of best lenses for portait photography.

Image bysvetlanasokolova

1. Canon EF 85mm f/1.2L II USM

The Canon EF 85mm f/1.2L II USM is a prime lens that's popular among portrait photographers for its ability to create a beautiful bokeh and shallow depth of field.

  • Pros
      wide maximum aperture of f/1.2
      creative control over depth of field
      The build quality is excellent
      image quality is sharp with beautiful colors and contrast
  • Cons
      relatively expensive
      it can be difficult to focus accurately at wide apertures.

2. Nikon AF-S Nikkor 85mm f/1.4G

The Nikon AF-S Nikkor 85mm f/1.4G is another popular prime lens for portrait photography, with a similar wide maximum aperture of f/1.4 for shallow depth of field and bokeh.

  • Pros
      excellent sharpness and contrast
      beautiful bokeh and creamy out-of-focus areas
      Fast and accurate autofocus
      Solid build quality
  • Cons
      relatively large and heavy

3. Sony FE 85mm f/1.8

The Sony FE 85mm f/1.8 is a prime lens designed for Sony full-frame mirrorless cameras, with a slightly narrower field of view compared to the previous options.

  • Pros
      Relatively affordable
      Excellent sharpness and contrast
      Fast and accurate autofocus
      Solid build quality
  • Cons
      Limited low-light performance

4. Tamron SP 70-200mm f/2.8 Di VC USD G2

The Tamron SP 70-200mm f/2.8 Di VC USD G2 is a telephoto zoom lens that's popular among portrait photographers for its versatility and ability to isolate the subject from the background.

  • Pros :
      The lens has a versatile focal range, allowing for both tighter portraits and full-body shots.
      The f/2.8 maximum aperture provides excellent low-light performance
      the image stabilization (VC) helps to reduce camera shake.
  • Cons :
      Relatively large and heavy

5. Sigma 50mm f/1.4 DG HSM Art.

The Sigma 50mm f/1.4 DG HSM Art is a prime lens with a wider field of view, making it great for environmental portraits and capturing more of the surroundings.

  • Pros
      The lens has a wide maximum aperture of f/1.4, allowing for great low-light performance and creative control over depth of field.
      Excellent sharpness and contrast
      Solid build quality
  • Cons
      Relatively large
