Best lenses for portrait photography

Portrait lenses are specifically designed to capture beautiful and flattering portraits of people. They typically have a wide aperture to create a shallow depth of field, which helps to separate the subject from the background and blur distractions plus they have a focal length that allows the photographer to capture the subject's face without distorting their features.

The key differences in photography elements when purchasing a portrait lens are :

  • Focal length : A lens with a focal length of 50mm or greater is typically preferred for portrait photography, as it will help to avoid distortion of facial features.
  • Aperture : A wide aperture, such as f/1.4 or f/1.8, will allow for a shallow depth of field, which can help to isolate the subject from the background and create a pleasing bokeh effect.
  • Image quality : Look for a lens with high-quality optics that will deliver sharp, detailed images.
  • Autofocus : A lens with fast and accurate autofocus will help to ensure that you can capture sharp images of your subject.
