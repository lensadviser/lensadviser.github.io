Top 5 Astrophotography Lenses

The list bellow shows the best lenses for astrophotography, based on low light performance, wide angle & sharpeness.

Image by wirestock

1. Sigma 20mm f/1.4 DG HSM Art

The Sigma 20mm f/1.4 DG HSM Art lens is considerer one of the best choices for astrophotography due to its wide-angle view and fast maximum aperture with 20mm focal length and a bright f/1.4 aperture, making it great for capturing both the night sky and landscapes.

  • Pros
      High-quality with minimal distortion and chromatic aberration
      Wide Angle
      Excellent brightness and bokeh
  • Cons
      relatively large

2. Canon EF 50mm f/1.8 STM Lens

The Canon EF 50mm f/1.8 STM lens is a prime lens with a narrow field of view, making it great for capturing individual stars or smaller portions of the night sky.

  • Pros
      lightweight and compact
      wide maximum aperture of f/1.8, allowing for decent low-light performance
      affordable
  • Cons
      The narrow field of view may not be suitable for capturing wider portions of the night sky or landscapes.

3. Sigma 14-24mm f/2.8 DG HSM Art

This lens is a great option for those looking for a wide-angle lens with excellent image quality and fast f/2.8 maximum aperture. The durable construction and weather sealing also make it suitable for outdoor photography.

  • Pros
      Ultra-wide-angle zoom lens.
      Fast HSM autofocus.
      Fast F2.8 maximum aperture
  • Cons
      Expensive.
      Relatively heavy.

To choose an astrophotography lens, consider the type of subjects you want to capture and the conditions of the night sky. Choose a lens with a wide aperture to allow more light in and a longer focal length for zooming in on distant objects. Look for lenses with good sharpness, minimal distortion, and low chromatic aberration.
