Best Underwater photography lenses

The list bellow shows the Top lenses for Underwater photography

Photo by Ayman Zaki

1. Nikon 105mm f/2.8G IF-ED VR Micro Lens

This macro lens is great for capturing close-up shots of marine life. Its VR (Vibration Reduction) system helps minimize camera shake, and the lens is compatible with Nikon's FX and DX format DSLRs.

  • Pros :
      Excellent sharpness and details.
      fast and accurate autofocus.
      great for capturing small subjects.
      VR system helps minimize camera shake.
  • Cons :
      may require additional accessories for underwater use

2. Canon EF 8-15mm f/4L Fisheye USM Lens

This fisheye lens is perfect for capturing wide-angle shots of the underwater world. It's compatible with both full-frame and APS-C Canon DSLRs.

  • Pros
      Captures a unique, distorted perspective.
      great for capturing wide-angle shots.
      fast and quiet autofocus
      versatile with both full-frame and APS-C cameras.
  • Cons
      may require a dome port for underwater use

3. Sony FE 90mm f/2.8 Macro G OSS Lens

This macro lens is designed for use with Sony mirrorless cameras and is great for capturing close-up shots of marine life.

  • Pros :
      Excellent sharpness and detail.
      fast and accurate autofocus.
      great for capturing small subjects.
  • Cons :
      may require additional accessories for underwater use

4. Canon EF 100mm f/2.8L Macro IS USM

The EF 100mm f/2.8L Macro IS USM has a true macro magnification ratio of 1:1, meaning the object you are photographing in real life is the same size as on your camera sensor. This versatile lens gives great results in portrait work and handheld movie-making, thanks to its ability to achieve a shallow depth of field with beautiful bokeh, along with built-in Hybrid Image Stabilization and lightweight design.

  • Pros
      Macro lens that's great for capturing small details and creatures.
      Image Stabilization (IS) helps reduce camera shake
      Good for capturing colorful creatures and coral
  • Cons
      Not a great option for capturing wider scenes or large creatures.

5. Olympus M.Zuiko Digital ED 60mm f/2.8 Macro Lens

This macro lens is designed for use with Olympus mirrorless cameras and is great for capturing small subjects up close.

  • Pros
      Compact and lightweight
      excellent image quality.
      great for capturing small subjects.
  • Cons
      Limited to Olympus cameras
      Relatively heavy.

To choose an underwater lens, consider the type of photography you want to do and the conditions of the underwater environment. Choose a lens with the appropriate focal length and make sure it's compatible with your camera. Look for lenses with underwater-specific features, such as a flat port or anti-reflective coatings.
